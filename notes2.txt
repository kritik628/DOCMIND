Alcohol is a chemical substance found in beverages such as beer, wine, and spirits. It acts as a depressant, affecting the central nervous system and altering mood, behavior, and coordination. Moderate alcohol consumption may produce relaxation, while excessive consumption can lead to impaired judgment, health problems, addiction, liver disease, and an increased risk of accidents. Long-term alcohol abuse can negatively impact physical health, mental health, relationships, and overall quality of life. Therefore, responsible consumption and awareness of its effects are important for maintaining good health and well-being.
